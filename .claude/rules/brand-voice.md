# Boredfolio Brand Voice

## Who We Are
The friend who actually understands finance and explains it to you at a house party — not the uncle who forwards "INVEST NOW" PDFs on WhatsApp.

## Tone DNA
- **Confident, not arrogant** — we know our stuff but we're not lecturing
- **Funny, not try-hard** — the joke serves the truth, never the other way around
- **Simple, not dumbed-down** — we use plain language because clarity is respect
- **Skeptical, not cynical** — we question the industry but we believe in investing

## Voice Modes

### Roast Mode (fund analysis, comparisons)
Sharp. Numbers-backed. The kind of honesty that makes fund managers uncomfortable.
- "Your fund charges 2.25% and can't beat Nifty. That's not wealth creation. That's a subscription to underperformance."
- Always pair the burn with a real number

### Teacher Mode (learn articles, explainers)
Patient. Clear. Like explaining to a smart friend, not a child.
- "A NAV of ₹500 doesn't mean the fund is expensive. It means nothing. Here's why..."
- Use analogies from daily life (food delivery, subscriptions, rent)

### Data Mode (live feeds, calculators, numbers)
Clean. Precise. Let the numbers do the talking.
- "₹50,000 Cr/year in fees. Whether they beat the index or not."
- Minimal copy. Maximum impact.

### Casual Mode (microcopy, tooltips, empty states)
Warm. Witty. Human.
- Loading: "Fetching all schemes from mfapi.in..." / "This takes a few seconds. We're downloading every scheme in India."
- Empty search: "Nothing found. Try a different spelling, or just browse."
- Error: "The API is having a moment. Try refreshing."

## The Screenshotability Test
Before shipping any copy, ask: "Would someone screenshot this and send it to their group chat?"
If no → rewrite.

## Banned Patterns
- "mutual funds sahi hai" energy
- LinkedIn-style inspiration ("Your portfolio is your future self thanking you")
- Jargon without translation
- Passive voice for things the industry actively does ("fees are charged" → "they charge you")
- Disclaimers that sound like we're scared (our disclaimer is honest and in plain English)
- Emojis in body copy (ok in microcopy sparingly)

## Numbers Rule
Every claim must have a real number behind it. We never say "funds charge high fees" — we say "funds charge ₹50,000 crore a year in fees." Specificity is credibility.
